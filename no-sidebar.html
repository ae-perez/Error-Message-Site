About Me

Now, this is the story all about how...

... my life got introduced to front-end development! A younger version of me, 11-year-old Alejandra to be exact, use to roam on the web, and discovered 't-dot wire'. Users on this site were able to use basic HTML code to include on their personal profiles and make it their own. I, unfortunately, lost touch with my little developer side, BUT upon completing my degree in Sociology, I was introduced again to programming by a friend. Ever since then I've 'become the princess of a town called Bel-Air'... kidding I've only been trying to become an expert of all things front-end!

At coffee shops and meetups is usually where I spend most of my days. Unfortunately, I’m no good at shootin’ b-ball, but if you’re willing to teach me, I’d be happy to learn! I also love sharing or listening to new creative ideas, I also wouldn't mind looking over code with you, to see which part of an app are great and which parts are up to no good. If by chance you’re movin’ to your auntie and uncle’s in Bel Air, or visiting Toronto, make sure you reach out, because you honestly never know, maybe we can teach each other something new!
